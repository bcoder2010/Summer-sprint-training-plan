Tuesday · Wednesday · Friday — Rest / Active Recovery
CNS recovery priority — avoid heavy loading
▼
Option A · Easy
20–30 min Walk
Flat surface, easy pace. Gets blood moving without loading the legs. Excellent for flushing out any DOMS from Monday's session. Best done morning or evening in a cool time slot — important for Tokyo heat.
Option B · Mobility
Hip & Posterior Chain Mobility Routine (20 min)
Hip flexor couch stretch — 2 min each side · 90/90 hip rotation — 1 min each · Pigeon pose or figure-4 — 2 min each · Lying hamstring stretch with band — 90 sec each · World's greatest stretch — 5 reps each side. These are the exact muscles you're loading Monday and Thursday — regular mobility here reduces injury risk significantly.
Option C · Pool
Swimming or Water Walking (30 min)
Zero-impact cardiovascular work. Water provides gentle compression that reduces inflammation. Easy laps or walking in the pool — not a swim workout, just movement. Ideal if you have pool access in summer.
Option D · Soft Tissue
Foam Roll + Massage Gun (15–20 min)
Focus on: glutes (sit on ball), hamstrings, calves, hip flexors, thoracic spine. Spend 60 seconds per area. The calves and hamstrings are your highest-risk pre-season muscles — don't skip them. Finish with 2 min of light ankle circles and toe spreading for Achilles health.
Saturday · Sunday — Full Rest
Sleep, eat, reset the CNS for next week
▼
Priority #1
Sleep 8–9 Hours
Most strength and speed adaptations happen during deep sleep. Human growth hormone peaks in slow-wave sleep, which is when muscle repair occurs. Aim for a consistent sleep time — irregular schedules reduce adaptation quality even if total hours are the same.
Priority #2
Nutrition Reset
Don't undereat on rest days — your body is still rebuilding. Aim for full protein targets (1.8–2.2g per kg bodyweight). Emphasise carbs in the evening meal to top up glycogen for Monday's session. Stay hydrated — muscle protein synthesis requires water.
Optional
Sunday Easy Walk or Stretch (15 min)
A 15-minute walk Sunday evening primes the CNS gently for Monday. Not required — skip if feeling fatigued. Never more than easy effort. If both days feel like low energy, that's a signal to check sleep and food quality before next week.

▼

No Training
In Phase 2, every training day is high-intensity. Cue System
Mechanics reference for every sprint session
0–30m · Acceleration
Drive, push, lean forward. Low body angle. Your strength.
Standing up too soon. Overstriding in drive.
30–60m · Transition
Gradually tall. Start thinking about stiffness. Arm drive active and compact.
Sudden posture change. Tensing up.
60–100m · Max Velocity
"SNAP UP · HOT GROUND · TALL & BOUNCE"
Pull foot off ground fast. Short, sharp contacts. No hip drop.
Pushing down. Overstriding. Sitting into hips. Grinding.
150–200m · Speed Endurance
Maintain tall posture. Arms drive harder when legs fatigue. Shorten stride, don't lose elasticity.
Tying up and pushing. Desperate overstriding at the end.
Elastic Stiffness Warmup
Before every track session. Focus on the sound — sharp tap, not a thud.

Heat Management
Tokyo July/August — non-negotiable protocols
🕐
Timing
Before 8am or after 6pm. Tokyo regularly hits 35°C+ with high humidity in July/August.
💧
Hydration
1.5L minimum at track. Start 500ml 30 min before. Electrolytes for sessions over 60 min.
🌡️
Over 35°C Rule
Cut sprint volume by 30%. Add 1 min to rest periods. 4 good reps > 6 bad ones.
🧊
Cooling
Wet towel on neck between reps. Ice pack on wrists/forehead. Don't sit in direct sun between reps.
⭐
Feel Ratings
Rate every session 1–5. Two consecutive sessions at 2 or below = take extra rest day.

Post-Sprint Recovery Shake
3 min · Post-track
▼
38g protein45g carbs~380 kcal
Ingredients
  • 1 scoop vanilla whey protein (30g protein)
  • 300ml whole milk or oat milk
  • 1 large banana
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp peanut butter
  • Handful of ice
Method
  1. Add all ingredients to blender
  2. Blend 30 seconds until smooth
  3. Drink within 30 minutes of finishing track session
💡 The banana + honey provides fast-absorbing carbs to replenish glycogen. The milk + whey covers your protein window. This is your most important post-track drink.

Sprint-Day Chicken Rice Bowl
25 min · Post-track or dinner
▼
48g protein65g carbs12g fat~580 kcal
Ingredients (1 serving)
  • 180g chicken breast or thigh
  • 150g cooked Japanese short-grain rice
  • 1 cup edamame (frozen)
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp ginger paste
  • Sesame seeds + spring onion to top
Method
  1. Marinate chicken in soy, sesame oil, and ginger for 10 min
  2. Pan fry or grill chicken 6–7 min each side
  3. Microwave edamame from frozen (3 min)
  4. Slice chicken and lay over hot rice
  5. Add edamame and avocado, drizzle leftover marinade
  6. Top with sesame seeds and spring onion
💡 This is the ideal post-track meal — fast carbs from rice, lean protein for muscle repair, healthy fats from avocado. Batch cook the rice and marinate chicken the night before for a 10-min assembly on track days.

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp honey

  • 200g firm tofu, cubed
  • 1 cup frozen edamame
  • 150g cooked rice
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp sriracha (optional)
  • Cucumber slices, shredded carrot, sesame seeds
Method
  1. Press tofu dry with paper towels, cube
  2. Pan-fry tofu in sesame oil until golden on all sides (8–10 min)

Meal Prep Chicken Thighs (6 portions)
35 min · Feeds Mon–Thu lunches
▼
Ingredients
  • 6 boneless chicken thighs (or breasts)
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger paste
Method
  1. Mix soy, honey, sesame oil, garlic, ginger into marinade
  2. Coat chicken and marinate 30 min minimum (overnight is better)

  4. Cool, slice or shred, divide into 6 containers
  5. Refrigerate up to 5 days
💡 The foundation of a week's worth of protein. Use in wraps, bowls, fried rice, noodles. Thighs stay juicy when reheated unlike breast. Batch every Sunday without fail.

Homemade Granola
25 min · 2-week supply
▼
22g carbs9g fat~190 kcal per portion
Ingredients
  • 300g rolled oats
  • 80g mixed nuts (almonds, cashews, walnuts)
  • 3 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 3 tbsp coconut oil, melted
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • Pinch of salt
  • Add after baking: 80g dried fruit, dark chocolate chips
Method
  1. Preheat oven to 170°C
  2. Mix oats, nuts, cinnamon, salt
  3. Add honey, coconut oil, vanilla — mix until coated
  4. Spread on lined tray
  5. Bake 20 min, stirring once at 10 min
  6. Cool completely, add dried fruit and chocolate
  7. Store in airtight jar up to 3 weeks
💡 Making your own granola saves money and lets you control the sugar content. Better than commercial granola which is often 30%+ sugar. Use on yoghurt, overnight oats, or açaí bowls all week.

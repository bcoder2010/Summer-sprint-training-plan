Cue System
Mechanics reference for every sprint session
0–30m · Acceleration
Drive, push, lean forward. Low body angle. Your strength.
Standing up too soon. Overstriding in drive.
30–60m · Transition
Gradually tall. Start thinking about stiffness. Arm drive active and compact.
Sudden posture change. Tensing up.
60–100m · MAX VELOCITY ⚡
"SNAP UP · HOT GROUND · TALL & BOUNCE"
Pull foot off ground fast. Short, sharp contacts. No hip drop.
Pushing down. Overstriding. Sitting into hips. Grinding.
150–200m · Speed Endurance
Maintain tall posture. Arms drive harder when legs fatigue. Shorten stride, don't lose elasticity.
Tying up and pushing. Desperate overstriding at the end.
Elastic Stiffness Warmup
Do this before EVERY track session. Focus on the SOUND — sharp tap, not a thud.
10 min easy jog + dynamic → Ankle pogos 2-ft (3×20) → Single-leg pogos (3×10 each) → High-freq A-skip (3×20m) → Wall drill fast alternating (3×10 each) → Build strides with snap cue (3×40m @ 85%)

Mid-Summer Check-in (Wk 11–12)
Heat Management
Tokyo July/August — non-negotiable protocols
🕐
Timing
Before 8am or after 6pm. Tokyo regularly hits 35°C+ with high humidity in July/August.
💧
Hydration
1.5L minimum at track. Start 500ml 30 min before. Electrolytes for sessions over 60 min.
🌡️
Over 35°C Rule
Cut sprint volume by 30%. Add 1 min to rest periods. 4 good reps > 6 bad ones.
🧊
Cooling
Wet towel on neck between reps. Ice pack on wrists/forehead. Don't sit in direct sun between reps.
⭐
Feel Ratings
Rate every session 1–5. Two consecutive sessions at 2 or below = take extra rest day. Humidity impairs performance even at moderate temps.
